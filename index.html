    User onboards to the application
    1 / 4
    📍 Touchpoints & User Challenges
    Touchpoints
    1. User logs into the partner app
    2. Applies to work as a Delhivery executive
    3. Adds Aadhaar details and completes the application
    4. User is prompted to download application and reach workplace
    5. User proceeds to add driving license, PAN card, bank details to configure their account on LM-FE App
    Challenges — Users
    1. Accessibility of progressive CTAs while onboarding on the partner app can be improved
    2. FE App can be shown as downloaded, but is not accessible in the downloads
    ⚠️ Design Challenges
    Challenges — Design
    1. Login and onboarding flows lack basic usability polishGhost text for phone number format is missing, error states use incorrect colors (yellow instead of red), and there's no option to go back and edit the phone number once entered. OTP fields are too small, and new users are taken directly to PIN setup without any intimation that they're new.
    2. Document verification and account setup is confusing and error-proneThe stepper for DL, PAN, and Bank Details is inconsistent — steps 1 and 2 are document uploads while step 3 is a form. Driving License is shown as a "Number" field when it's actually a 15-digit alphanumeric code. PAN-Aadhaar linking copy needs refinement, and there's no help or guidance if users don't have their cards linked.
    3. The landing page and post-login experience fails to orient the userThe page hierarchy is off — "Good morning" is prioritized over pending document verification. The landing page has only one CTA with no other relevant workflow details. Concepts like "LastMile Pro" and "permission to auto-start" are not explained. There's no primary navigation — everything is hidden under a menu.
    4. No multilingual support or accessibility featuresUsers with lower literacy levels, which is critical for the target user base, have no language options or accessibility accommodations.
    📊 Metrics to Track
    Metrics to Track
    Onboarding Completion Rate
    % of riders who start the Partner App signup and successfully complete all steps (DL, PAN, bank details) to become active on the FE App.
    First-week Attrition Rate
    % of riders who leave within 7 days of onboarding. If riders don't understand the app, they churn before generating any value.
    Document Verification Error Rate
    % of document submissions that fail on first attempt (DL format errors, PAN-Aadhaar linking failures, bank verification failures).
    Support Ticket Volume During Onboarding
    Number of help requests raised in the first 7 days. If this is high, the app isn't self-explanatory enough.
    ✦ Opportunity Areas
    Opportunity Areas
    1. Redesign the login and document verification flow as a single guided experienceA unified, linear onboarding wizard with proper input masks, inline validation, and clear progress indication would directly reduce the document verification error rate and first-week attrition.
    2. Build a meaningful post-login landing experienceRestructuring the landing page to surface pending actions, explain key concepts, and provide primary navigation would reduce support ticket volume and help new riders orient themselves faster.
    3. Introduce multilingual support and accessibility accommodationsAdding regional language options and simplified UI patterns (icon-led navigation, voice guidance) would improve onboarding completion rate across Tier 2/3 cities.

    1. Shipment list lacks critical information hierarchyNumber of packages, proximity from current location, and potential earnings are secondary or missing. Users can't easily filter by priority.
    2. Route optimization is absentThere's no shortest-path guidance for multiple delivery points. Riders have to manually find directions for each location one by one.
    3. Create dispatch flow has navigation and status gapsHow a user navigates from the homepage to the shipment/dispatch screens is unclear. "Punch out" is not at the same hierarchical level as "create dispatch."
    4. Iconography and visual cues are inconsistentThe lightning icon's purpose is unclear, amount tags are inconsistently shown, and the shipment/summary icons look off in terms of size and weight.

    ✦ Opportunity Areas
    Opportunity Areas
    1. Redesign the shipment list with an information hierarchy that supports decision-makingSurfacing earnings potential, distance, and shipment type with inline education would reduce dispatch creation time and the learning curve for new riders.
    2. Introduce route optimization for multiple delivery pointsEven a basic route suggestion (ordered delivery sequence based on proximity) would reduce average delivery time per shipment and dwell time.
    3. Fix the create dispatch and punch-in/punch-out flow hierarchyEqualizing the hierarchy of daily-use actions and fixing state management would streamline the DC-to-field transition.

    2. Rethink delivery verification to close loopholes while reducing frictionResequence verification (selfie upfront), close self-signature loophole, add alternative verification methods.
    3. Build a reliable cancellation flow with fallback mechanismsSMS/WhatsApp/in-app OTP fallbacks and clear post-cancellation status.
    4. Address the map-to-actual-location discrepancy activelyAllow riders to flag and correct addresses, show customer-provided landmarks, enable quick call/chat before arrival.
    5. Move app updates to off-peak hoursSchedule updates to non-delivery hours or enable background updates.

    Onboarding through Partner App
    1 / 4
    📍 Touchpoints & Heuristic Findings
    Touchpoints
    1. Rider downloads Partner App and creates account
    2. Submits identity documents (Aadhaar, DL, PAN)
    3. Completes background verification process
    4. Gets approved and is directed to download Hyperlocal app
    5. Logs into Hyperlocal app with registered credentials
    Heuristic Issues
    1. Phone number input field lacks format guidance and ghost text
    2. OTP input fields are undersized for touch targets
    3. Error states use inconsistent color coding across screens
    4. No back navigation to edit phone number after submission
    5. New user vs returning user flow is not differentiated
    ⚠️ Design Challenges
    Challenges — Design
    1. Two-app onboarding creates a fragmented experienceRiders must navigate between Partner App and Hyperlocal App with no clear handoff. The transition point is confusing — riders don't know when to switch apps or what's expected in each. Document submission happens in one app while account configuration happens in another.
    2. Document upload flow lacks inline validationDL number format is not validated in real-time. PAN card upload doesn't confirm successful capture. Bank details form has no auto-fill or IFSC lookup. Users discover errors only after submission, requiring re-upload.
    3. Login screen has multiple usability gapsPhone number field has no country code selector or format mask. OTP auto-read is unreliable. PIN setup screen appears without context for new users. No biometric login option despite being a field app used multiple times daily.
    4. Post-login landing doesn't surface pending setup tasksAfter first login, riders see a generic homepage instead of a setup checklist. Pending verifications are buried in settings. No progress indicator showing how close they are to being "active."
    5. No accessibility considerations for diverse rider baseSingle language (English), no text scaling, no high-contrast mode. Hyperlocal riders in Tier 2/3 cities need regional language support and simplified visual patterns.
    📊 Metrics to Track
    Metrics to Track
    Partner-to-Hyperlocal Conversion Rate
    % of riders who complete Partner App signup and successfully activate on Hyperlocal App. The two-app transition is a major drop-off point.
    Document Rejection Rate
    % of document submissions rejected on first attempt. No inline validation means riders discover errors only after submission.
    Time to First Trip
    Duration from account creation to completing the first delivery trip. Longer times indicate onboarding friction.
    Day-1 to Day-7 Retention
    % of riders still active after first week. Early churn signals onboarding confusion and unmet expectations.
    ✦ Opportunity Areas
    Opportunity Areas
    1. Unify the two-app onboarding into a seamless guided flowCreate a clear bridge between Partner App and Hyperlocal App with deep links, progress persistence, and a visual checklist that works across both apps. Riders should never wonder "what do I do next?" during setup.
    2. Add real-time document validation with smart captureUse OCR for DL/PAN capture, auto-fill IFSC from bank name, validate formats inline before submission. Reduce the document rejection rate by catching errors at the point of input, not after server-side processing.
    3. Build a first-time user experience with setup progress trackingReplace the generic post-login homepage with a setup wizard showing completion percentage, pending tasks, and estimated time to become active. Surface this until all verifications are complete.
    📍 Touchpoints & Heuristic Findings
    Touchpoints
    1. Rider opens Hyperlocal app and views homepage
    2. Sees available trips and earnings summary
    3. Navigates to different sections via bottom nav or hamburger menu
    4. Views notifications and alerts
    5. Checks slot availability and booking status
    6. Accesses quick actions (start trip, view wallet)
    Heuristic Issues
    1. Homepage information density is overwhelming with no clear visual hierarchy
    2. Critical actions (start trip, view earnings) compete for attention equally
    3. Navigation between bottom tabs and hamburger menu is inconsistent
    4. Notification badges don't clear reliably after viewing
    5. Popup modals interrupt workflow without clear dismiss patterns
    6. Status indicators use inconsistent color coding across sections
    ⚠️ Design Challenges
    Challenges — Design
    1. Homepage tries to show everything, prioritizes nothingEarnings, trips, slots, notifications, and promotions all compete for the same viewport. There's no clear primary action. Riders have to scan the entire screen to find what they need — whether it's starting a trip or checking their balance.
    2. Navigation model is split between bottom tabs and hamburger menuSome features are in bottom tabs, others are hidden in the hamburger menu. There's no consistent logic for what goes where. Wallet is in the menu but trips are in tabs. This forces riders to learn two navigation patterns.
    3. Popup and modal management disrupts workflowPromotional popups, permission requests, and system alerts appear without clear hierarchy. Some modals don't have obvious dismiss buttons. Stacked modals create a "popup hell" experience on first login.
    4. Real-time status updates are not surfaced effectivelyTrip assignments, slot confirmations, and payment updates don't have a unified notification center. Riders miss critical updates because they're scattered across different screens.
    📊 Metrics to Track
    Metrics to Track
    Time to Primary Action
    How long it takes a rider to perform their most common action (start trip, check earnings) from app open. A cluttered homepage inflates this.
    Navigation Depth per Session
    Average number of taps to reach key features. Split navigation (tabs + hamburger) increases this unnecessarily.
    Popup Dismiss Rate
    % of popups dismissed without interaction. High rates indicate popups are interrupting rather than informing.
    ✦ Opportunity Areas
    Opportunity Areas
    1. Redesign homepage as a context-aware mission controlShow different homepage states based on rider context: pre-trip (show available trips + earnings potential), during-trip (show active delivery + navigation), post-trip (show summary + next actions). One primary CTA per state.
    2. Consolidate navigation into a single, consistent modelMove all primary features to bottom tabs with clear icons and labels. Use the hamburger menu only for settings and secondary features. Reduce navigation depth for daily-use features to 1-2 taps maximum.
    3. Implement a smart notification center with priority queuingReplace scattered popups with a unified notification feed. Queue non-urgent notifications. Show critical alerts (trip assignment, payment received) as inline banners, not blocking modals.
    4. Add contextual quick actions based on time of day and rider stateMorning: show slot booking + trip availability. During shift: show active trip + earnings counter. End of day: show summary + wallet balance. Reduce cognitive load by showing only what's relevant.
    📍 Touchpoints & Heuristic Findings
    Touchpoints
    1. Rider receives trip assignment notification
    2. Views trip details (pickup location, drop location, items)
    3. Navigates to pickup point using in-app maps
    4. Confirms pickup with store/restaurant
    5. Navigates to delivery location
    6. Completes delivery with customer verification
    7. Marks trip as complete and views earnings
    Heuristic Issues
    1. Trip assignment notification doesn't show estimated earnings upfront
    2. Map integration has lag between location updates
    3. Pickup confirmation flow requires too many taps
    4. Customer contact options are buried during active delivery
    5. Trip completion screen doesn't show cumulative daily earnings
    ⚠️ Design Challenges
    Challenges — Design
    1. Trip assignment lacks decision-support informationRiders see pickup/drop locations but not estimated earnings, distance, or time. They can't make informed accept/reject decisions. No comparison with alternative trips. Acceptance timeout pressure without adequate information leads to blind acceptance or rejection.
    2. In-trip navigation is disconnected from delivery workflowMap view and delivery details are on separate screens. Riders constantly switch between navigation and order info. No integrated view showing route + order details + customer contact in one place.
    3. Pickup and delivery confirmation flows are over-engineeredMultiple confirmation steps at pickup (arrive → verify items → confirm pickup) and delivery (arrive → verify customer → collect payment → confirm delivery). Each step is a separate screen with loading states. For a time-sensitive workflow, this adds 30-60 seconds per delivery.
    4. Multi-order trip management is confusingWhen assigned multiple pickups/deliveries, the sequencing logic isn't clear. Riders can't easily see which order to handle next. No visual distinction between pickup and delivery stops on the map.
    5. Error recovery during trips is poorIf a customer is unavailable, the "mark as undeliverable" flow is buried. If the app crashes mid-trip, resuming requires re-navigating to the active trip. No offline mode for areas with poor connectivity.
    6. Real-time trip status updates to customers are not rider-visibleRiders don't know what status the customer sees. This leads to mismatched expectations — customer thinks rider is "arriving" while rider is still at pickup.
    📊 Metrics to Track
    Metrics to Track
    Trip Acceptance Rate
    % of assigned trips accepted by riders. Low rates indicate poor trip information or unfavorable assignments.
    Pickup-to-Delivery Time
    Average time from pickup confirmation to delivery completion. Over-engineered confirmation flows inflate this.
    App Switches During Active Trip
    Number of times riders switch to external maps or phone dialer during a trip. High counts indicate in-app tools are insufficient.
    Trip Cancellation Rate (Post-Accept)
    % of trips cancelled after acceptance. Often caused by inadequate trip info at assignment time.
    Customer Complaint Rate per Trip
    % of trips generating a customer complaint. Mismatched status updates and delivery delays are primary drivers.
    ✦ Opportunity Areas
    Opportunity Areas
    1. Show earnings estimate and trip details upfront at assignmentDisplay estimated earnings, total distance, estimated time, and number of stops before the rider accepts. Allow comparison with current location. This directly improves trip acceptance rate and reduces post-accept cancellations.
    2. Build an integrated trip view combining map, order details, and actionsA single screen showing the route map with order details in a bottom sheet, customer contact as a floating button, and confirmation actions as swipe gestures. Eliminate the constant screen-switching during active deliveries.
    3. Streamline pickup and delivery confirmation to single-action flowsReplace multi-step confirmation with swipe-to-confirm or single-tap actions. Use location-based auto-detection for arrival confirmation. Reduce per-delivery overhead from 30-60 seconds to under 10 seconds.
    4. Add clear multi-order sequencing with visual route guidanceFor multi-stop trips, show a numbered sequence on the map with color-coded pickup (blue) and delivery (green) markers. Auto-navigate to the next stop after each completion. Show estimated time for remaining stops.
    5. Implement offline mode and crash recovery for active tripsCache active trip data locally. If the app crashes or loses connectivity, auto-resume to the exact trip state on relaunch. Queue confirmation actions for sync when connectivity returns.
    📍 Touchpoints & Heuristic Findings
    Touchpoints
    1. Rider checks wallet balance and transaction history
    2. Views payout schedule and pending settlements
    3. Books weekly slots for delivery shifts
    4. Raises support tickets for trip or payment issues
    5. Views past trip history and earnings breakdown
    6. Manages profile settings and documents
    Heuristic Issues
    1. Wallet balance doesn't distinguish between available and pending amounts
    2. Slot booking calendar doesn't show earnings potential per slot
    3. Support ticket status updates are not pushed as notifications
    4. Trip history lacks filtering and search capabilities
    ⚠️ Design Challenges
    Challenges — Design
    1. Wallet lacks transparency on money flowRiders can't see the breakdown of earnings vs deductions vs pending settlements. Transaction history shows amounts but not context (which trip, which bonus). Payout schedule is not clearly communicated — riders don't know when they'll get paid.
    2. Slot booking is a calendar without intelligenceRiders see available slots but not which slots have higher demand (and therefore higher earnings potential). No recommendation for optimal slot selection. Fully booked slots show no waitlist option. Cancellation policy for booked slots is unclear.
    3. Support system is reactive, not contextualRiders must navigate to a separate support section, select a category, describe the issue from scratch. No auto-attachment of trip details or screenshots. No estimated resolution time. Ticket status requires manual checking — no push notifications.
    4. Trip history is a flat list with no analyticsPast trips are shown chronologically with basic details. No earnings trends, no performance insights, no comparison across weeks/months. Riders can't learn from their own data to optimize their work patterns.
    5. Settings and profile management is buried and incompleteDocument updates (expired DL, new bank account) require navigating deep into settings. No proactive alerts for expiring documents. Vehicle details and insurance information management is missing or hard to find.
    6. Incentive and bonus structures are opaqueRiders know bonuses exist but can't see clear progress toward them. Milestone thresholds are not displayed. Bonus calculations after payout don't match rider expectations because the rules aren't transparent.
    7. No offline access to critical informationWallet balance, upcoming slots, and active support tickets are not cached. In areas with poor connectivity, riders can't access basic information they need for planning.
    📊 Metrics to Track
    Metrics to Track
    Payout Query Rate
    % of riders who contact support about payment issues. High rates indicate wallet transparency problems.
    Slot Utilization Rate
    % of booked slots where riders actually show up and complete trips. Low utilization suggests poor slot selection guidance.
    Support Resolution Time
    Average time from ticket creation to resolution. No contextual auto-fill and manual status checking inflate this.
    Rider Retention at 30/60/90 Days
    Long-term retention correlated with wallet transparency, earnings predictability, and support quality.
    ✦ Opportunity Areas
    Opportunity Areas
    1. Build a transparent wallet with real-time earnings breakdownShow available balance, pending settlements, and upcoming payout date prominently. Each transaction should link to its source trip with full context. Add a "projected earnings this week" based on booked slots and historical data.
    2. Add intelligence to slot booking with demand-based recommendationsShow estimated earnings per slot based on historical demand. Recommend optimal slot combinations for maximum earnings. Add waitlist for fully booked high-demand slots. Show cancellation policy clearly before booking.
    3. Implement contextual, in-flow support with auto-populated detailsAllow raising support tickets from within trip details or wallet transactions with auto-attached context. Show estimated resolution time. Push ticket status updates as notifications. Add a quick-help FAQ layer before ticket creation.
    4. Transform trip history into an actionable performance dashboardAdd weekly/monthly earnings trends, average trip time, peak earning hours, and performance comparisons. Help riders identify their most profitable patterns. Show incentive progress and bonus milestones clearly.
    5. Proactive document and profile management with alertsPush notifications for expiring documents 30/15/7 days before expiry. One-tap document re-upload from the alert. Surface vehicle insurance and registration renewal reminders. Keep profile management accessible from the main navigation.
    6. Make incentive structures transparent with real-time progress trackingShow bonus milestones as a progress bar on the homepage. Display exactly how many more trips/hours are needed to hit the next bonus tier. After payout, show a clear breakdown of base earnings + incentives + deductions.
